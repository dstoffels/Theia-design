## Settlements, Structures, Stations

A settlement is a network of **structures** within defined **borders**. They are established by **Civic Centers** that allow its owners to govern the settlement. Borders are initially defined by the area of effect of its Civic Center, expanded by building additional structures within existing borders, each having their own area of effect. These borders dictate who can build, where structures can be placed, and political jurisdiction, where laws and policies are enforced.

Ownership belongs to the the **Stewards** of the settlement, citizens that reside in the Civic Center as public servants. Stewards have administrative access to the settlement, allowing them to manage resources, structures, and other denizens. Each Civic Center has a limited number of Steward slots, which may be increased by upgrading the Civic Center. The founder of the settlement automatically becomes a steward, and all stewards can add other characters as stewards, citizens or denizens. They can each step down and the last remaining steward can abandon the settlement, leaving it to decay or be reclaimed by anyone.

Characters can also petition to become **denizens** or **citizens** of a settlement at its civic center. A character may only be a citizen of one settlement at a time, but a denizen of multiple settlements. Denizens can own private structures or rent housing within a settlement, but don't have access to communal facilities or resources. Denizens and citizens must also petition at the civic center to build new structures in a settlement and can purchase any publicly owned structures that may be for sale. Only citizens count toward the settlement's population, which is what allows the settlement to expand and upgrade. 

**Structures** provide housing, research stations, crafting stations, resource management and commerce. They're destructible and require resource upkeep for persistance or they'll slowly decay over time. When built, a structure can be designated **public**, **communal** or **private**, which dictates ownership, access and upkeep responsibility. Public and communal structures are owned and maintained by the settlement, but only citizens can access communal structures. Private structures are owned and maintained by individual denizens and citizens, who control access.

**Stations** are interactive nodes for crafting, research, trade, resource management and other automated activities. Some stations are intrinsic to a structure, while others are **improvements** that can be added to upgrade the structure, incurring additional upkeep. Stations can be **public**, **communal** or **private**, just like structures.

### Civic Centers
Civic Centers are multifunctional structures that establish a settlement. Only one can be built per settlement and they provide administrative stations for stewards to manage with the settlement. They provide housing slots, crafting stations, civic stations, limited resource stations for storage, and a central marketplace where players can rent stalls to buy and sell items. Civic Centers upgrade to larger, more advanced structures to grow the settlement and unlock new structures, stations and improvements.

#### Campsite
A campsite is the first, most basic type of structure and can be built anywhere outside settlement borders. It provides minimal, unsecured storage, housing for 4 characters and tier 1 crafting. A campsite can be set up anywhere in the world, but is a temporary structure that can be easily destroyed or looted. It establishes the ***Camp*** settlement when built and can be the initial foundation of a permanent settlement or a temporary base of operations that can be abandoned to decay.

**Stations**
- **Campfire**: a basic, tier 1 crafting station, primarily for Survival.
- **Bedroll (4)**: crude housing, providing minimal homestead bonus, unsecured storage and a respawn point for the assigned character.

#### Encampment

An encampment is a simple wooden shelter for 4 citizens and secured storage, allowing for tier 2 crafting. The settlement perimeter can now be fortified with palisades, while leaving room for other structures within. An encampment can act as a temporary base of operations, but is more durable than a campsite and can be maintained for longer periods of time.

Constructing the encampment upgrades your settlement to a ***Homestead***, providing the **homestead bonus** to citizens and allowing you to add other players to your settlement. It unlocks the ***Longhouse*** when the citizen population reaches 8 (requires 4 huts).

##### New Structures:
- ***Hut***: a small, single-room dwelling that provides basic shelter and storage for one citizen. 
- ***Garden***: a small plot for cultivating basic crops and herbs, which can be used for food or alchemical ingredients.
- ***Corral***: a small, round enclosure for corralling beasts of burden and producing food resources.

#### Longhouse
| Settlement | Predecessor |  Successor | Improvements | Capacity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hamlet | Encampment | Moot Hall | None | 8 |

A longhouse is a large, partitioned house that expands the settlement perimeter and introduces the first governmental controls. It unlocks civil service, allowing its owner to add other players as settlement administrators. It also adds eminent domain, the ***relocating*** and ***repurposing*** of structures, which returns the resources used to build the structure to its owner. Constructing the longhouse upgrades your settlement to a ***Hamlet*** and unlocks the ***Moot Hall*** upgrade when the settlement population reaches 16.

##### New Structures
- ***Cottage***: a three-room dwelling that provides shelter and storage for two citizens. 
- ***Tannery***: a facility for processing animal hides into leather for tier 3 crafting (Accoutrements).
- ***Worksite***: a specialized construction area for tier 3 Builder crafting.
- ***Plantation***: a large agricultural site for cultivating larger crops on a larger scale.
- ***Paddock***: a large enclosure for corralling more livestock and producing food resources on a larger scale.

---

#### Moot Hall
| Settlement | Predecessor | Successor | Improvements | Capacity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Village | Longhouse | Guildhall | Stone walls | 8 |

The moot hall is a large, two story stone building that introduces law and culture. The hall houses a jail for apprehended outlaws and characters can serve as guards. Stone walls can now be constructed. The settlement may now accept new citizens publicly, players can petition for policies and citizenship. Constructing the moot hall upgrades your settlement to a ***Village*** and unlocks its successor, the ***Guildhall*** when the settlement population reaches 32.

##### New Structures
- **House**: a large dwelling that provides shelter and storage for up to four citizens, which can be rented out to generate income for the owner.
- **Inn**: a multi-room building that can generate income for its owner by renting out rooms and providing supplies for visitors.
- **Forum**: a large public space for discourse and spiritual enlightenment for mystics and scholars.
- **Clothier**: a textile facility and shop for crafting tier 3 Accoutrements.
- **Grove**: a large plot for cultivating trees.

---

#### Guildhall

| Settlement | Predecessor | Successor | Improvements | Capacity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Town | Moot Hall | Keep | Portcullises, Battlements, Turrets | 8 |

The guildhall is a grand, multi-story building, the civic heart of a large settlement and a symbol of its success. Laws and policies can now be enacted and enforced with guards and jail cells within the structure. Districts can be partitioned within the settlement. Trade routes can now be established with other settlements. The guildhall itself and existing fortifications can be upgraded with Portcullises, Battlements, and Turrets. Constructing the guildhall upgrades your settlement to a ***Town*** and unlocks its successor, the ***Keep*** when the settlement population reaches 64.

##### New Structures
- ***Tenement***: a dense, multi-room rowhouse that provides shelter and storage for 16 citizens that can be rented out to generate income for the owner.
- ***Smithy***: a facility for tier 4 crafting in metallurgy.
- ***Workshop***: a facility for tier 4 crafting in woodworking and stonemasonry.
- ***Laboratory***: a facility for tier 4 crafting in alchemy.
- ***Academy***: a public institution for enchanting and elemental research for mages and scholars.

---

#### Keep

| Settlement | Predecessor | Successor | Improvements | Capacity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| City | Guildhall | None | None | 16 |

The keep is a massive, fortified castle that serves as the pinnacle of a settlement's development. Constructing the keep upgrades your settlement to a ***City***, the largest and most advanced settlement.

##### New Structures
- ***Manor***: a grand, ornate residence that provides shelter and storage for up to 8 citizens, which can be rented out to generate income for the owner.
- ***Focal Spire***: a towering magical structure for tier 5 crafting and research in spellbinding and consecration and the creation of permanent portals.

### Dwellings
Dwellings provide housing for citizens, where they can securely store personal belongings and easily swap out kits. Dwellings provide a safe place to rest.

### Resource
Structures that provide a place to generate and store materials.

### Facilities
Facilities are used for crafting and refining materials.

### Academic
Academic structures are used for research and learning.

## Stations
Stations are interactive nodes for crafting, research, trade, resource management and other automated activities. Some stations are intrinsic to a structure, while others are **improvements** that can be added to upgrade the structure, incurring additional upkeep. Stations can be **public**, **communal** or **private**, just like structures.

**Furnaces**
- **Kiln**: a basic furnace for smelting ores into metal ingots and firing ceramics, made of clay and stone.
- **Smelter**: an advanced furnace for smelting ores into metal ingots and firing ceramics, made of stone and metal.
- **Bloomery**: a furnace for smelting iron and steel.
- **Draft Furnace**: a furnace for smelting high-quality steel alloys, made of stone and metal, with a bellows improvement for increased efficiency.
- **Mana Forge**: a magical furnace for smelting mithril and rolaren, made of stone and enchanted with mana, with a bellows improvement for increased efficiency.
